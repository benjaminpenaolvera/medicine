Top Medical Specialties

Plastic Surgery
Plastic Surgery

Plastic surgery is a medical specialty involving operations which focus on the correction of the congenital process that requires repair or replacement due to the effects it has on the form and/or function of the human body. Mexico is currently the third leading-country for cosmetic plastic surgery in the world. The most common procedures are liposuction, mammoplasty (breast augmentation), and rhytidectomy (face lift).

The field can be broken down into two main categories:

Reconstructive plastic surgery

Cosmetic plastic surgery

Restores or betters the function and corrects deformities caused by birth defects, trauma, or medical conditions and are typically considered medically necessary. Examples include cleft lip and palate repair, breast reconstruction, and surgery after burn injuries.

Targets primarily healthy patients interested in correcting physical alterations with the goal of obtaining a more refined facial and corporal structure. The repercussions pertain mainly to the mental and emotional stability of the patient.

Cardiac Surgery
Cardiac Surgery

Cardiac surgery, also known as heart surgery, corrects life-threatening conditions by performing surgical operations on the heart which, depending on the condition, can either be open-heart surgery or minimally invasive surgery. Cardiovascular diseases are the leading cause of death in the U.S., examples include heart defect repair, coronary artery bypass, and the use or implantation of ventricular assist devices (VADs).

Ophthalmology
Ophthalmology

Ophthalmology is the branch of medical science that studies the structure, functions, and diseases of the eye and corrects any abnormalities. The leading causes of blindness and low vision in the U.S. are primarily age-related diseases such as glaucoma, macular degeneration, cataracts, and diabetic retinopathy.

Oncology
Oncology

Oncology is the medical specialization that focuses on the diagnosis and treatment of cancer. The field can be broken down into three main categories: medical oncology, radiation oncology, and surgical oncology. Medical Oncology uses chemotherapy, hormone therapy and other medicinal options to treat cancer. Radiation Oncology treats cancer with controlled exposure of the human body to radiation. Surgical Oncology treats cancer through surgery and other medical procedures.

Fertility
Fertility Treatments

According to the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, approximately 12% of women in the U.S. ages 15-44, regardless of marital status, present problems getting pregnant and/or carrying a baby to term. One third of infertility cases are caused by infertility problems in men. Determining which treatment is required depends on whether the reproductive problem is present in one or both partners but the two most common treatment options are artificial insemination, that has an effectiveness percent rate of 15-20%, and egg donation, with an 80% effectiveness rate.

Gastrointestinal Surgery
Gastrointestinal Surgery

Gastroenterology is a branch that focuses on the evaluation, diagnosis, planning and surgical practices that cures disorders of the digestive system. Gastrointestinal surgery is the surgical practice that treats and cures these disorders in the esophagus, stomach, small and large intestine, gallbladder, liver, and pancreas.

Traumatology
Traumatology

Traumatology is the branch of medicine that focuses on the study, treatment, and prevention of physical injuries to the muscular system, which includes muscles, bones, ligaments, tendons and any related tissues. In most cases, these traumas are caused by accidents, such as a car wreck, and violent situations, like a gunshot wound. Other traumas can be caused by sports-related incidents.

Gynecology
Gynecology

Gynecology is the branch of physiology and medicine that focuses on the diagnosis and treatment of the diseases and disorders affecting the female body, primarily the reproductive system. Examples of disorders include endometriosis, interstitial cystitis, and uterine firbroids.

Odontology
Odontology

Odontology is the medical specialty that focuses on the study of the diseases and structure of the teeth and their surrounding structure as well as the treatment, cure, and prevention of disorders. Examples include cavities, gingivitis and gum disease.
